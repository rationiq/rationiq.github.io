Under the GDPR, the six lawful bases for processing personal data include: Consent: When an individual clearly agrees to their data being processed for specific purposes. Contract: When processing is needed for a contract with an individual or for actions taken before a contract. Legal Obligation: When processing is required to meet a legal duty under EU or Member State law. Vital Interests: When processing is essential to save someone's life, usually in urgent situations. Public Task: When processing is necessary for a public interest task or official duties with a legal basis. Legitimate Interests: When processing is necessary for legitimate interests, provided these are not overridden by the individual's rights.
