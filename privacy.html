4. Legal Bases for Processing (For GDPR-Covered Users)

 

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), any processing of personal data must be justified by at least one of six specific lawful bases, as outlined in Article 6 of the GDPR. The appropriate basis depends on the specific purpose and context of the processing activity, and organizations must document their chosen basis and include it in their privacy notice. Under the GDPR, the six lawful bases for processing personal data include:

 

Consent: When an individual clearly agrees to their data being processed for specific purposes.

Contract: When processing is needed for a contract with an individual or for actions taken before a contract.

Legal Obligation: When processing is required to meet a legal duty under EU or Member State law.

Vital Interests: When processing is essential to save someone's life, usually in urgent situations.

Public Task: When processing is necessary for a public interest task or official duties with a legal basis.

Legitimate Interests: When processing is necessary for legitimate interests, provided these are not overridden by the individual's rights.

 

Where applicable, we rely on:

 

Consent

Legitimate interests (e.g., service improvement, security)

Legal obligations
